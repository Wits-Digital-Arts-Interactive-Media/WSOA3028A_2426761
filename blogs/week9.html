Ok. Let me start off by saying these readings were somewhat long and made some fairly interesting points about the access to information and the internet across the globe. The first textual discussed how there’s an uneven spread of power and information access across the world. Let’s take a look at South Africa for example regarding this topic. South Africa is one of many countries that does not cater to the majority in terms of having access to internet. The internet access across South Africa is limited to a select few that can afford internet/wifi. Giving more people access to internet in South Africa could be majorly beneficial for businesses, education, economics, and general improvement of South Africa as a whole. Let’s not even get into the whole dispute about access to power. The loadshedding crisis in South Africa affects so much regarding access to the internet and information. If this is remedied, there can be further developments in ensuring access to information and power is achieved across the entire country. As mentioned in the reading, for information to flow in and out of certain areas, infrastructure, socio-economic conditions and cultural context needs to be taken into consideration. These aspects are what makes areas so unique and introducing access to information to these places, needs to be done to ensure there is some conservation and preservation of these things.

This reading goes into detail about how there is a digital divide between those with access to information, and those without. The article discusses how this contributes to inequality with access to information resources across religions and socio-economic groups. The cultural differences in South Africa are very vast and the lack of information across the country affects the ability to teach, share and include people in these various cultures, traditions and societies. Information reduces ignorance, and I think, despite South Africa knowing as much as they do about different cultures, learning more about certain traditions and practices would do South Africa, and the rest of the world, a lot of good. Power and control is also something mentioned in the paper that is quite vital in that, the difference in accesses to information contributes to power dynamics in information flow. Understanding these things is essential for ensuring there is no misuse of digital rights, privacy and freedom of expression. This is vital for the world we currently live in and will only become that more important with the several advances in technology being made. Ensuring people understand their rights to these things mentioned, is important for the user’s protection and for overall knowledge of how the digital world works and how to interact with it. Having access to information helps with networking and can get people, businesses and companies far. The lack of information can cause stagnation in development and growth in many factors, and as a South African, I’m sure of many ways the access of information can help our country.

The second reading speaks about the relationship between geography and the internet and how they influence each other. The paper discusses how physical infrastructure of the internet is influenced by geography. Things like cables, data centres and network nodes are taken into consideration based on how they can be maintained and developed regarding geographical factors like topography and climate. Taking this into consideration for South Africa, I think that we have a fairly good topology for these infrastructures. With regards to climate, I think South Africa has a particularly interesting climate. All the physical infrastructure may have issues regarding the heat during South Africa’s summers and springs, however this can be remedied by implementing colling systems. Similar to the first paper, the second paper talks about how there can be urban and rural development with access to high-speed internet, and how it can create many opportunities and increase the quality of life amongst communities in both urban and rural areas. This would do great in South Africa as there is a vast combination of rural and urban communities throughout the country. Introducing the access to information and internet across both these groups, will improve their opportunities and help improve opportunities and their way of life. It will also create a bridge between the two area, which will give insight to either side and a platform to start providing help where necessary.

Again, the discussion on interactions and social exchange is mentioned here in the second reading.
