Values of Play: Design Tradeoffs in Society-Oriented Game Design.

This extract discusses what is referred to as the “RAPUNSEL project”, which is a game environment that teaches middle-school girls how to programme. This project came about because of the concern that the interest in maths, science and computing has declined among younger females. The game creates a dancing environment, in which the girls have to programme characters to create dance sequences using JavaScript. The reading talks about how the game doubles as a way to teach the girls social skills, creative and novel engagement with technology. I think this is phenomenal and is such a good way to integrate teaching and learning through games. This approach to teach the girls how to code is so good because it’ll be an easier way for them to learn, rather than just seeing code and having to create things without any goals. This game addresses the issue of gender imbalances in the STEM environment and will help to improve these problems for the future.

The extract also talks about battling with integrating values into the design process and how they managed to come up with an idea on how to do this and what challenges they would be faced with. Here are some of the significant value’s dimensions: The game is aimed to challenge gender biases and empower young girls in technology, which makes it a politically charged game. The creators also got input and involvement from teachers, parents, and industry reps, to help integrate feedback with varying political, social and ethical views. Character representation was also considered as games can often raise questions about gender, race stereotypes, social rewards, and interaction style.

The Methodologies for integrating these values: they are working on designing systems that embody shared values, which is presented through the technology design. They encourage practitioners to engage in reflective conversations about the design problems between beliefs and actions. They look into playtesting and iterative reflection from the users and ask for feedback based on user preference and cultural values.

It is so important for companies creating games that have a diverse audience to take all of these things into account because of how many users it may affect. Gaming is one of the easiest ways to learn, so if there are issues in the design of the game that teaches people incorrect values, those aspects of the game must be addressed and dealt with in order to create a safer learning environment. This idea of the RAPUNSEL project is such a great concept and will really add value to the technological and digital world. As a girly in STEM, who was also not really exposed to much technology and coding at a young age, I genuinely feel like this will work wonders for girls around the world and give them another option to consider for careers they might want to pursue.

A Game Design Methodology to incorporate Social Activist Themes

This article is sort of similar to the one above, in that their focus is on game design for women and the exploration of educational and activist game approaches. They discuss the importance of games and their cultural significance, which can reinforce values and beliefs, whether intentional or not. There is an important point they make about how games can embody both positive and negative themes. They use “The Sims” as an example of consumerism and “Grand Theft Auto” for stereotypes. The point they are making is that game designers need to intentionally embed values and ethical thinking into their design to ensure users are socially conscious. The RAPUNSEL project (as discussed above) is mentioned to be a good example of a game that has a goal to teach under privileged girls how to programme, and simultaneously promote values of gender equity. The mention of the RAPUNSEL project is to draw from their practices and goals to design games that influence industry practices and create more communal and inclusive games.

I think what this extract is trying to achieve is so meaningful and important because future generations will definitely be more inclined to learning through games and it is extremely vital to ensure that what people are learning from games besides understanding and gaining knowledge is to attain good morals and views about life, as well as equipping them with important skill sets that will aid them for their futures.
