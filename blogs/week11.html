Analysis of the readings:

‘The road to Technocolonialism’ talks about the geopolitical implications of artificial intelligence development. The article says that the main countries that dominate the artificial intelligence growth is America and China. Their changes and development in artificial intelligence has a major influence and impact on the world. The talk about techno-colonialism comes from the idea that the powers of AI can extend their political and economical powers over the world. There is a fear that AI can be exchanged for political influence and access to information. This is terrifying because the AI tool will be used for malicious means rather than the development and benefit of the world. This development could lead to the control and dominance over certain countries, particularly third world countries, which will result in a technological colonialism.

The article gives some examples of how technocolonialism is already in play, as China and America increase their influence onto certain African countries through economic dependence, data collection, control of digital ecosystems and much more. This shows that there can be extensive progress in technocolonialism as African countries become more dependent on China and America for AI technology. What’s stopping them from doing this across other continents too? Having countries depend on China and America for technology and AI tools, gives China and America the upper hand on power and places them in a huge position of control. I know I said this above, but I want you to understand the extent of how problematic this is. The colonialism with regards to technology in a technological world will cause major divide and a difference in accessibility across all countries with and without access to technology and the internet. It’s crazy to think that we’ve (mostly) escaped colonialism but there are ways in which it finds its way back… and through technology and access to information?? I can’t imagine why the world we live in is full of people who always want the upper hand and cannot help the next person. Ensuring that people are treated equally with regard to access to information and access to technology, should not have to raise concerns about who controls those things and who benefits most from it. Helping people with technology and information in this technological age should be second nature.
